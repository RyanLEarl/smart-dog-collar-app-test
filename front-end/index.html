Purpose

This project seeks to develop a smart dog collar prototype that detects seizures in dogs. Leveraging this accelerometer and gyroscope data, machine learning models can be trained and deployed to detect seizures in real-time. The collar will be connected to an app for the owner so they can monitor their dogs behaviours and receive notifications of seizures in real-time. This allows instant action to be taken to mitigate the negative effects of a seizure. Current goal is to design and build a dozen devices to be used in a formal research study.

Back Story

  • Mabel (b. 2013)
    • rescue ¾ Greyhound, ¼ Irish Wolfhound
    • developed epilepsy in 2017
    • “cluster seizure” for ~12 hours
    • vets could not stop it
    • brain temperature reached 109°F
    • suffered brain damage (“hard drive wipe”)
    • slowly relearned to stand, walk, bathroom, etc.
    • she enjoys a great life as of 2022
    • If I had had this proposed technology, I could have intervened earlier and avoid the worst of this.

Project Value

When looking on the market for smart dog collars, many offer products that track normal dog activity such as sleeping, walking, and running. What we aim to provide is a product that does both that and provides the detection of seizures in dogs which is not available in any current products. This will help owners feel at ease when they cannot be with their pets in their moment of need.
